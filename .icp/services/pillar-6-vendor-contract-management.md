## PAGE POSITIONING STATEMENT

Churches are consistently oversold by technology vendors. Not because vendors are malicious — but because churches buy without a technical advocate, and vendors sell to buyers who can't evaluate what they're purchasing.

The evidence is systemic:
- Churches routinely overpay their MSPs by **40–60% above market rate** — because no one with technical expertise reviewed the contract before it was signed (industry estimate, ongoing)
- Organizations waste an average of **$21 million annually** on unused SaaS licenses — a figure that scales proportionally to every church's software budget (Zylo SaaS Management Index, 2025)
- **$34 billion** is wasted annually on unused software licenses in the US and UK (G2, 2025)
- **62% of organizations** were audited by a major software vendor in 2024, a significant increase from 40% in 2023 (Business Wire, 2025) — churches are not exempt
- AV capital projects routinely exceed budget and underperform specifications when there is no independent technical reviewer of the vendor's proposal

The pattern is consistent: a technology vendor presents a proposal to a church leadership team. The leadership team has ministry expertise, financial discipline, and institutional commitment — but no technical depth to evaluate what they're being sold. They ask good questions. The vendor answers in vendor language. The contract gets signed.

Good Shepherd Insights changes that dynamic. We are the technical expert in the room that churches have never had — evaluating every vendor proposal, reviewing every contract, benchmarking every pricing model, and negotiating on behalf of the church. With zero financial interest in any vendor outcome.

**The market context:**
- Churches overpay MSPs **40–60% above market rate** (industry estimate)
- **76% of companies** over-license software to avoid vendor penalties (G2, 2025) — churches do the same, without the legal sophistication to push back
- SaaS license waste rose **14.2% year-over-year** in 2024 (Zylo, 2025)
- The average church runs **5–8 software tools** with no centralized license management
- AV vendor proposals for church capital projects are rarely reviewed by an independent technical expert before signing

Every dollar a church overpays for technology is a dollar not funding ministry. GSI recovers that stewardship.

#### What It Is
IT Vendor & MSP Evaluation is a structured engagement to assess the church's current IT vendor or Managed Service Provider relationship — evaluating contract terms, pricing, performance, and market fit — and, where appropriate, managing an RFP process to identify better-performing, better-priced alternatives.

The managed services market for churches is opaque. Most MSPs serving churches charge rates based on what the church will accept, not what the market bears. Without independent technical expertise to benchmark and evaluate, churches consistently overpay. GSI provides the market knowledge, technical vocabulary, and negotiation leverage that corrects that imbalance.

#### Who It's For
- Church Business Administrators reviewing an MSP contract renewal
- Finance Committees questioning the value of current IT support spending
- Executive Pastors who inherited an MSP relationship and don't know if it's appropriate
- Churches that have experienced poor MSP performance — slow response times, repeated unresolved issues, poor communication — and want an independent evaluation before renewing or transitioning

#### What GSI Does
1. **Current Contract Review** — Line-by-line review of the existing MSP agreement: scope of services, service level agreements, response time commitments, escalation paths, renewal terms, auto-renewal clauses, termination provisions, and data ownership

5. **Negotiation Support** — Where the church is renewing with the current MSP or selecting a new one, GSI negotiates on the church's behalf: pricing, scope, SLA standards, termination terms, and transition provisions
6. **Transition Management** — For churches changing MSPs: GSI manages the transition process — knowledge transfer, system documentation handoff, credential transfer, and new MSP onboarding — protecting continuity of IT operations

#### Outcomes
- A clear, independent assessment of whether the church's current MSP contract is appropriate
- Market-rate benchmarking that gives the church negotiating leverage
- An RFP process managed professionally — not dependent on the church's internal technical capacity
- A negotiated contract that reflects the church's interests, not the vendor's defaults
- Transition management that protects operational continuity when changing providers

### 6.2 — Software License & SaaS Audit

**Page Title:** Church Software License & SaaS Audit | Good Shepherd Insights
**Meta Description:** GSI audits church software licenses and SaaS subscriptions — identifying unused licenses, duplicate tools, over-provisioned seats, and consolidation opportunities that reduce technology spend.

**SEO / AEO Keywords:**
- church software audit
- church SaaS audit
- church software license review
- church technology spend audit
- church subscription management
- unused church software licenses
- church software consolidation
- church technology cost reduction

#### What It Is
A Software License & SaaS Audit is a structured review of every software subscription and license the church is paying for — assessing actual utilization against license counts, identifying duplicate capabilities across tools, right-sizing license tiers to actual usage, and generating a renewal calendar that allows the church to make intentional decisions instead of reactive auto-renewals.

Organizations waste an average of **$21 million annually on unused SaaS licenses** (Zylo, 2025). For churches, the proportional equivalent represents a significant stewardship failure — money leaving the ministry budget for software nobody uses, serving nobody the church is trying to reach.

#### Who It's For
- Church Business Administrators and Finance Committees responsible for the technology budget
- Executive Pastors who want to understand what the church is spending on software and whether it's justified
- Churches preparing for a budget cycle and needing accurate technology spend data
- Churches that have experienced rapid staff growth — and the software sprawl that accompanies it

#### What GSI Does
1. **Full SaaS Inventory** — Complete catalog of every software subscription the church is paying for: platform, tier, seat count, annual cost, renewal date, and primary users
2. **Utilization Assessment** — For each subscription, an assessment of actual utilization: how many licensed seats are active, which features are being used, and what percentage of the subscription's value is being realized
3. **Duplicate Capability Identification** — Identification of tools that perform overlapping functions — where the church is paying for the same capability twice across two different platforms
4. **License Right-Sizing** — Recommendations to reduce seat counts, downgrade tiers, or eliminate tools entirely — matched to actual current usage rather than historical assumptions
5. **Renewal Calendar with Action Recommendations** — A 12-month renewal calendar showing every contract renewal date, recommended action (renew, renegotiate, replace, cancel), and anticipated cost impact
6. **Consolidation Recommendations** — Where appropriate, specific recommendations to consolidate tools onto fewer platforms — reducing vendor management complexity and achieving volume pricing

#### Outcomes
- A complete, accurate software inventory the church has likely never had before
- Identification of unused licenses and duplicate tools generating immediate savings opportunities
- A renewal calendar that prevents the passive, unconsidered auto-renewals that waste ministry budget
- Right-sized license tiers that reflect current usage rather than overly cautious historical provisioning

#### Why This Matters
Most churches have never conducted a formal SaaS audit. The result is a technology budget that grows year-over-year through passive auto-renewals, vendor upsells, and departmental software purchases nobody centrally tracks. The first audit almost always produces immediate cost savings that exceed the cost of the engagement.

#### Fractional CTO Transition Hook
Software inventory management and license governance are permanent operational functions. As new tools are adopted, staff join and depart, and vendors change pricing, the license landscape requires continuous oversight. GSI's Fractional CTO engagement maintains the software inventory and governs the renewal calendar on an ongoing basis.
