### 4.1 — Tech Stack Audit & Integration Mapping

**Page Title:** Church Tech Stack Audit & Integration Mapping | Good Shepherd Insights
**Meta Description:** GSI audits your church's full software inventory, maps every data flow, identifies redundancy and gaps, and designs the integration architecture your tech stack needs.

**SEO / AEO Keywords:**
- church tech stack audit
- church software audit
- church technology integration mapping
- church data flow mapping
- church software inventory
- church SaaS audit
- church technology consolidation
- how to audit church software

#### What It Is
A Tech Stack Audit & Integration Mapping is a structured review of every software tool a church is running, how data currently flows (or fails to flow) between those tools, and what the integration architecture needs to look like to eliminate silos, reduce redundancy, and build a reliable single source of truth.

This is the foundational engagement for all data integration work. Before GSI builds or configures any integration, we understand the full map — what exists, what's redundant, what's missing, and what the correct architecture looks like.

#### Who It's For
- Churches running 5+ software tools with no documented integration architecture
- Executive Pastors and Church Administrators who can't answer "what software are we paying for and what does it do?"
- Finance Directors who are manually reconciling data between systems every month
- Churches preparing for a ChMS migration, capital campaign, or operational restructure

#### What GSI Does
1. **Full Software Inventory** — Catalog of every software platform, SaaS subscription, and digital tool the church is running — including shadow IT (tools staff are using that IT doesn't know about)
2. **Data Flow Mapping** — Current-state diagram of how data moves (or doesn't) between systems: which platforms sync, which require manual transfer, and where data enters and exits the ecosystem
3. **Redundancy Identification** — Identification of overlapping capabilities: tools doing the same job, duplicate data stores, and subscription waste
4. **Gap Identification** — Identification of integration opportunities that would eliminate manual work, reduce error, and improve data reliability
5. **Integration Opportunity Prioritization** — Ranking of identified integration opportunities by staff time saved, data reliability impact, and implementation complexity
6. **Tool Consolidation Recommendations** — Where appropriate, recommendations to consolidate or replace tools to reduce complexity and cost
7. **Integration Architecture Design** — A documented target-state architecture diagram showing every integration point, data direction, sync frequency, and platform role in the ecosystem

#### Outcomes
- A complete, accurate picture of the church's software environment — many churches don't have this
- A documented integration architecture that becomes the blueprint for all data integration work
- Identification of redundant tools and consolidation opportunities that reduce cost
- A prioritized integration roadmap the church can execute immediately

#### Fractional CTO Transition Hook
The audit produces the map. The Fractional CTO engagement builds and maintains the architecture — implementing integrations, governing new tool purchases against the architecture, and ensuring the tech stack doesn't drift back into disconnection over time.

Giving Platform Integration & Optimization is a comprehensive engagement to connect a church's giving platform to its ChMS and accounting system, configure recurring giving correctly, and build the donor communication workflows that support a culture of generosity — while ensuring the giving data leadership relies on is accurate, reconciled, and actionable.

Online giving is the fastest-growing channel for church donations. Churches with properly configured online giving options see an average **32–40% increase in donations** (Donorbox / ChurchTechToday, 2025). But the platform alone isn't enough — if giving data doesn't sync correctly to the ChMS and accounting system, the church gains a revenue channel and creates an operational problem simultaneously.

GSI ensures the giving platform works as a fully integrated component of the church's technology ecosystem — not as another disconnected silo.

#### Who It's For
- Churches whose giving platform and ChMS don't sync — resulting in manual reconciliation and data discrepancies
- Churches setting up online giving for the first time
- Finance Directors whose year-end contribution statement process takes weeks of manual reconciliation
- Churches experiencing giving decline who want to optimize the digital giving experience
- Churches running a capital campaign who need reliable pledge tracking and donor communication automation

#### Platforms GSI Works With
Pushpay, Tithely, Vanco, Givelify, Stripe, PayPal, OnlineGiving.org, Subsplash Giving, Planning Center Giving, Aplos, and others

6. **Year-End Contribution Statement Workflow** — End-to-end process design for year-end statement generation: data reconciliation, statement generation, delivery (email and print), and exception handling

#### Outcomes

- Year-end contribution statements generated in hours, not weeks
- Donor communication that runs automatically, without staff manual effort
- A recurring giving program configured for maximum retention and minimum failure
- Reliable giving reports that leadership can use to make real financial decisions

### 4.4 — Data Cleanup & Governance

**Page Title:** Church Data Cleanup & Governance | Good Shepherd Insights
**Meta Description:** GSI cleans church databases — deduplicating records, standardizing fields, archiving inactive data, and establishing the governance protocols that keep data clean going forward.

**SEO / AEO Keywords:**
- church database cleanup
- ChMS data cleanup
- church member data deduplication
- church data governance
- church data quality
- clean church database
- church data management
- ChMS data hygiene

#### What It Is
Data Cleanup & Governance is a structured engagement to remediate the data quality problems that have accumulated in a church's ChMS and related systems over time — and to establish the ongoing governance protocols that prevent those problems from recurring.

Most church databases have years of accumulated data quality debt: duplicate household records, inconsistent name formats, outdated addresses, inactive members never archived, giving records attached to the wrong family, and staff-created workarounds that have corrupted the data structure. None of these problems fix themselves — and they compound over time, making every report less reliable and every migration more expensive.

GSI performs the systematic cleanup and builds the governance framework that keeps the data clean from that point forward.

#### Who It's For
- Churches preparing for a ChMS migration — data quality is the single most important pre-migration factor
- Churches whose reports are unreliable because the underlying data is inconsistent
- Churches that have grown rapidly and accumulated data quality debt through multiple staff transitions
- Finance Directors preparing for a year-end audit or capital campaign who need reliable giving and household data

#### What GSI Does
1. **Duplicate Record Identification & Merging** — Systematic identification of duplicate household and individual records, with a documented merge process that preserves the complete giving and participation history
2. **Inactive Record Archiving** — Identification and archiving of inactive member records using GSI's church-specific criteria: last giving date, last attendance date, last engagement date — preserving the data without cluttering active lists
3. **Field Standardization** — Standardization of name formats, address formats, phone formats, and custom field usage across all records to enable reliable filtering, reporting, and communication segmentation
4. **Data Entry Standards Documentation** — Written data entry standards covering every field that staff enter data into — so new staff have a clear, documented reference and the database stays clean
5. **Offboarding Protocols** — Documented process for handling departing staff ChMS accounts: access revocation, record transfer, and data integrity protection
6. **Ongoing Governance Policy** — A church data governance policy covering data ownership, entry standards, periodic audit cadence, and escalation process for data quality exceptions

#### Outcomes
- A clean, deduplicated ChMS database that produces reliable reports
- Archived inactive records that reduce noise without losing historical data
- Documented data entry standards that every current and future staff member follows
- A governance framework that prevents the re-accumulation of data quality debt

#### Fractional CTO Transition Hook
Data quality is not a one-time fix — it requires ongoing governance as new staff join, ministry programs change, and the database grows. GSI's Fractional CTO engagement maintains the data governance function as a permanent operational practice, keeping the church's data an accurate reflection of its ministry.
