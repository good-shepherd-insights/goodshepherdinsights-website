#### What It Is
ChMS Selection & Evaluation is a structured, independent process that helps churches identify, evaluate, and confidently choose the right church management software platform — without being driven by vendor relationships, peer pressure, or incomplete information.

With more than 30 viable ChMS platforms on the market, each with different pricing models, feature sets, integration capabilities, and ministry fit profiles, the selection process is genuinely complex. #### Who It's For
- Churches currently running on spreadsheets, paper, or a legacy system no longer fit for purpose

- Churches that are about to launch a new campus, hire new staff, or significantly restructure operations

1. **Ministry Needs Assessment** — Structured discovery covering church size, ministry complexity, staff roles, integration requirements, and a 3-year growth trajectory

5. **Risk Assessment** — Each platform evaluated for implementation risk, data migration complexity, vendor stability, and contract terms
6. **3-Year Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Modeling** — True cost comparison including platform fees, implementation, training, integration, and support
7. **Final Recommendation Report** — Board-ready documentation with a clear recommendation, rationale, risk flags, and a transition readiness checklist

#### Outcomes

- Time saved — a process that typically takes churches 6–12 months internally, completed in 4–8 weeks

### 1.2 — ChMS Migration

**Page Title:** Church Management Software Migration | Good Shepherd Insights

#### What It Is
ChMS Migration is full-scope project management and oversight for churches moving from one ChMS to another — or from paper/spreadsheets to a ChMS for the first time. It covers every phase from pre-migration audit through post-launch validation, with GSI acting as the church's technical advocate throughout.

The most common ChMS migration failures stem from three preventable causes: inadequate data cleaning before the move, poor change management with staff, and no independent oversight of the vendor's migration process. GSI closes all three gaps.

#### Who It's For
- Churches transitioning between any two ChMS platforms
- Churches implementing a ChMS for the first time
- Churches that have had a previous migration fail — partially or completely
- Executive Pastors who need someone else to own the project without losing control of the outcomes

#### Common Migration Scenarios GSI Manages
- Breeze → Planning Center Online
- Fellowship One → Rock RMS
- ACS/Realm → MinistryPlatform
- ChurchTrac → Planning Center
- Paper/Excel → Any ChMS
- ShelbyNext → Realm
- Custom/Legacy → Cloud-based ChMS

#### What GSI Does
1. **Pre-Migration Audit** — Full review of current data: completeness, accuracy, duplicate records, field mapping readiness, and export feasibility
2. **Data Mapping** — Precise field-by-field mapping from source system to destination system, including custom fields, giving history, household structures, and group membership
3. **Data Cleaning** — Deduplication, standardization, and archiving of inactive records before any migration begins. No bad data transferred.
4. **Migration Project Management** — GSI manages the vendor timeline, holds the vendor accountable to the migration plan, and protects the church from scope creep and hidden costs
5. **Parallel Running Oversight** — During the transition period when both systems are active, GSI establishes protocols to prevent data divergence
6. **Post-Migration Validation** — Systematic verification that all records transferred correctly: member counts, giving history totals, household links, group memberships, check-in history
7. **Staff Cutover Training** — Role-based training for every staff member affected by the new system before go-live

#### Outcomes
- A clean, complete data migration with no lost records, no duplicate households, and validated giving history
- Staff who are trained and confident in the new system on Day 1
- A vendor relationship that GSI has managed — meaning the church isn't learning the hard lessons of implementation alone
- On-time, on-budget delivery

#### Why This Matters
The biggest ChMS migrations fail not because of technology but because of people and process. Broken household relationships, missing giving history, failed recurring giving transfers — these are the recurring failure modes GSI has been built to prevent.

#### Fractional CTO Transition Hook

That's not a one-time project — it's an ongoing operational requirement. GSI's Fractional CTO engagement provides the sustained leadership to ensure the new platform delivers its full value over time.

### 1.3 — ChMS Integration

**Page Title:** Church Management Software Integration | Good Shepherd Insights
**Meta Description:** GSI connects your ChMS to your giving platform, accounting software, email system, website, and more — building a unified church tech stack that actually works together.

**SEO / AEO Keywords:**
- ChMS integration services
- connect Planning Center to QuickBooks
- church software integration
- ChMS API integration
- church tech stack integration
- Planning Center Pushpay integration
- church management software and giving platform sync

#### What It Is
ChMS Integration is the architectural and operational work of connecting a church's ChMS to the rest of its technology stack — so data flows cleanly between systems, manual re-entry is eliminated, and leadership has a single source of truth for member data, giving records, and ministry activity.

The average church runs **5–8 disconnected software tools** (practitioner consensus, 2023). Without integration, each tool becomes a data silo — giving data doesn't match ChMS records, email communications don't reflect current group membership, check-in data doesn't feed attendance reports. The result is staff time wasted on manual reconciliation, and leadership making decisions on unreliable data.

#### Who It's For
- Churches whose ChMS and giving platform don't sync — resulting in data discrepancies and manual workarounds
- Churches running 5+ software tools with no integration architecture
- Operations Directors and Church Administrators tired of double data entry
- Churches preparing for a capital campaign, building project, or growth initiative where data reliability is critical

#### Common Integration Scenarios GSI Builds
- ChMS ↔ Giving Platform (Pushpay, Tithely, Vanco, Givelify, PCO Giving)
- ChMS ↔ Accounting (QuickBooks, Aplos, Xero)
- ChMS ↔ Email/Communication (Mailchimp, Constant Contact, Text In Church, PastorsLine)
- ChMS ↔ Website (member directory, event calendar, sermon archive)
- ChMS ↔ Child Check-In
- ChMS ↔ Streaming & Attendance
- ChMS ↔ Background Check (Ministry Safe, Checkr)
- ChMS ↔ Small Group Management

#### What GSI Does
1. **Tech Stack Audit** — Full inventory of every software tool the church is running and how data currently flows (or doesn't) between them
2. **Integration Mapping** — Architecture diagram showing every integration point, data direction, sync frequency, and field mapping
3. **Native Integration Configuration** — Where platforms offer built-in connectors, GSI configures and tests them
4. **API / Zapier / Make Integration Design** — For platforms without native connectors, GSI designs and builds automations using API connections or integration platforms
5. **Data Flow Testing** — End-to-end testing of each integration: trigger a giving transaction, verify it appears correctly in ChMS and accounting
6. **Ongoing Health Monitoring** — Integration failures are silent killers. GSI establishes monitoring protocols so broken syncs are caught before they cause reporting problems

#### Outcomes
- Elimination of manual data re-entry across integrated systems
- A single, reliable member record as the source of truth
- Giving data that matches between the giving platform, ChMS, and accounting
- Staff hours reclaimed from reconciliation tasks

#### Fractional CTO Transition Hook
Integrations require maintenance. Platforms update APIs, vendors change field structures, and new tools get added to the stack. GSI's Fractional CTO engagement provides ongoing integration governance — so the church's tech stack doesn't drift back into disconnection over time.

### 1.5 — ChMS Hosting & Infrastructure

#### What It Is
ChMS Hosting & Infrastructure is a specialized service for churches running self-hosted or on-premise church management software — most commonly Rock RMS, PowerChurch, or legacy ACS installations. It covers hosting environment assessment, cloud migration planning, vendor selection, backup and disaster recovery, and ongoing security hardening.

While most modern ChMS platforms are SaaS-based, a significant number of churches — particularly larger congregations and those running Rock RMS — still manage their own hosting infrastructure. This creates security exposure, operational risk, and maintenance burden that most churches are not equipped to manage without dedicated IT leadership.

#### Who It's For
- Churches running Rock RMS on local servers or self-managed hosting
- Churches on legacy ACS or PowerChurch installations
- Churches experiencing performance issues, downtime, or data loss events related to their hosting environment
- Multi-site churches whose self-hosted infrastructure hasn't scaled with campus growth

#### What GSI Does
1. **Hosting Environment Assessment** — Current-state review of server hardware, OS, database configuration, uptime history, backup integrity, and access controls
2. **Cloud Migration Feasibility Study** — For churches considering a move to managed cloud hosting for Rock RMS or similar: options analysis, cost modeling, risk assessment
3. **Hosting Vendor Selection** — Independent evaluation and selection of managed hosting providers with church technology experience
4. **Backup & Disaster Recovery Planning** — Implementation of documented, tested backup procedures and a clear recovery time objective (RTO) for system restoration
5. **Security Hardening** — Patch management, access control review, remote access security (VPN/RDP hardening), and endpoint security for servers and admin workstations
6. **Ongoing Monitoring** — Establishing uptime monitoring, backup verification alerts, and a regular infrastructure health review cadence

#### Outcomes
- A secure, reliable hosting environment that doesn't depend on a volunteer IT person to keep running
- A documented disaster recovery plan that the church has tested
- Reduced operational risk and clear vendor accountability for hosting performance

#### Fractional CTO Transition Hook
