Your church deserves a clear technology direction — not scattered decisions driven by vendor pitches. Church Technology Strategy gives you a strategic roadmap aligned with your ministry goals, so every technology investment serves your mission instead of draining your budget.

Most mid-sized churches make technology decisions reactively — a vendor calls, a tool expires, a crisis hits. Without a plan, those decisions accumulate into a stack of tools that overlap, underperform, and cost more than they should. A Technology Strategy Audit gives you a professional assessment of your current tech stack, identifies gaps and risks, and produces a strategic roadmap that prioritizes your needs based on your mission.

## What You Get [.h2 .font-bold .font-secondary]

- A full inventory of your current technology tools and how they connect
- Identification of gaps, overlaps, and security risks in your current setup
- A prioritized roadmap aligned with your ministry goals and budget
- Clear recommendations for what to keep, what to replace, and what to consolidate
- Ongoing partnership to keep your strategy current as your church grows

## Why This Matters

Churches that operate without a technology strategy spend 40–60% more on vendor contracts than necessary and average 5–8 disconnected tools that duplicate effort. Church Technology Strategy turns scattered decisions into a coherent plan — so you invest in technology that serves your ministry, not technology that creates more work for your staff.
