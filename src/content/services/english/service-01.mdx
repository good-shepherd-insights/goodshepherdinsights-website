The ChMS market is large, fragmented, and vendor-driven. With over 30 active platforms competing for your congregation’s budget, most evaluation processes are shaped by vendor sales teams, not independent advisors. The global ChMS market was valued at $851 million in 2025 and is growing at 8.6% annually—yet fewer than 60% of US churches have adopted any platform, and of those that have, fewer than 40% use the full feature set they pay for. Good Shepherd Insights changes that equation. As the only Fractional CTO practice built exclusively for churches, we bring zero platform allegiance to every engagement. We don’t resell software or earn referral fees. We evaluate, recommend, implement, integrate, and optimize every major ChMS platform—Planning Center, Rock RMS, Breeze, Realm, MinistryPlatform, Elvanto/Tithely, ChurchTrac, Fellowship One, TouchPoint, ParishSOFT, Subsplash, and more—with one objective: the right platform for your ministry context, at the right total cost. Whether you are a multi-site church or a single campus, we bridge the gap between pastoral vision and technical execution, ensuring your staff isn’t just using software, but leveraging a mission-critical tool.

Churches don’t fail at ChMS because they chose the wrong software. They fail because there is no structured evaluation process—decisions made by committee, vendor relationship, or price. They fail because of poor data hygiene before migration, no integration planning between the ChMS and the giving platform or accounting system, and underutilization after go-live when staff revert to spreadsheets because no one trained them. They fail because a 1,500-member multi-site church ends up on Breeze, or a 150-member single campus on MinistryPlatform—wrong platform, wrong context. GSI addresses every layer of this failure chain. From ChMS selection and evaluation through migration, integration, optimization and training, to hosting and infrastructure for self-hosted platforms like Rock RMS—we provide the disciplined, data-driven methodology that replaces committee chaos with clear, board-ready recommendations and protects your member records and giving history from the silent killers of data divergence and underutilization.

## Eliminating the Most Expensive Technology Mistakes in Church Operations [.h2 .font-bold .font-secondary]

ChMS implementation failures are among the most costly mistakes churches make. GSI eliminates them with a comprehensive advisory practice covering five critical areas. **ChMS Selection & Evaluation**: a structured, independent process with ministry needs assessment, feature comparison matrix, vendor demo facilitation, 3-year TCO modeling, and board-ready recommendation—completed in 4–8 weeks instead of the 6–12 months churches typically spend internally. **ChMS Migration**: end-to-end oversight from pre-migration audit and data mapping through deduplication, vendor management, parallel running, post-migration validation, and role-based staff training—whether moving from Breeze to Planning Center, Fellowship One to Rock RMS, or paper to your first platform. **ChMS Integration**: connecting your ChMS to Pushpay, Tithely, QuickBooks, Aplos, Mailchimp, and the rest of your tech stack so data flows cleanly and giving records match across every system. **ChMS Optimization & Training**: closing the gap between what you pay for and what you use through utilization audits, workflow reconfiguration, and role-based staff training on your real data. **ChMS Hosting & Infrastructure**: assessment, cloud migration, security hardening, and disaster recovery for churches running Rock RMS or PowerChurch on local servers. Every engagement surfaces the full scope of a church’s technology needs—and the natural progression is a Fractional CTO retainer, so the church never faces a major technology decision alone again.
