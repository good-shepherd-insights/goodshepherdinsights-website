## ChMS Selection & Evaluation [.h2 .font-bold .font-secondary]

Choosing the right church management software is one of the most consequential technology decisions your church will make — and most churches make it without a technical advocate. GSI's ChMS Selection & Evaluation service provides a structured, independent process that replaces committee chaos with clear, data-driven recommendations. We run a disciplined evaluation methodology: ministry needs assessment, platform shortlist development, feature comparison matrix, vendor demo facilitation with a pre-set question framework that cuts through sales theater, risk assessment covering implementation risk and data migration complexity, 3-year total cost of ownership modeling, and a board-ready final recommendation report. This is for churches currently running on spreadsheets or legacy systems, churches that have outgrown their current ChMS, and executive pastors responsible for the technology decision who lack the technical depth to evaluate platforms independently. A process that typically takes churches 6–12 months internally, GSI completes in 4–8 weeks — eliminating the most common source of ChMS implementation failure at its root.

## ChMS Migration [.h2 .font-bold .font-secondary]

ChMS migration failures are among the most costly technology mistakes churches make — typically caused by inadequate data cleaning before the move, poor change management with staff, and no independent oversight of the vendor's migration process. GSI manages end-to-end ChMS migrations, protecting your data, managing your vendor, training your staff, and making sure your new platform launches clean. Our process covers every phase: pre-migration audit of current data completeness and accuracy, field-by-field data mapping including custom fields and giving history, deduplication and standardization before any migration begins, vendor timeline management with accountability for scope creep and hidden costs, parallel running oversight to prevent data divergence during transition, post-migration validation of all records and giving history, and role-based staff cutover training before go-live. Whether you're moving from Breeze to Planning Center, Fellowship One to Rock RMS, ACS/Realm to MinistryPlatform, or from paper and spreadsheets to your first ChMS, GSI acts as your technical advocate throughout — ensuring no lost records, no duplicate households, and validated giving data on Day 1.

## ChMS Integration [.h2 .font-bold .font-secondary]

The average church runs 5–8 disconnected software tools. Without integration, each tool becomes a data silo — giving data doesn't match ChMS records, email communications don't reflect current group membership, check-in data doesn't feed attendance reports. GSI connects your ChMS to your giving platform, accounting software, email system, website, and more — building a unified church tech stack that actually works together. We begin with a full tech stack audit and integration mapping, then configure native connectors where platforms offer them and design API or Zapier/Make automations where they don't. Every integration is end-to-end tested — trigger a giving transaction, verify it appears correctly in ChMS and accounting. We also establish monitoring protocols so broken syncs are caught before they cause reporting problems. Common integrations include ChMS to Pushpay, Tithely, or Vanco for giving; QuickBooks, Aplos, or Xero for accounting; Mailchimp or Text In Church for communications; and child check-in, background checks, streaming, and small group management platforms. The result: elimination of manual data re-entry, a single reliable member record as the source of truth, and giving data that matches across every system.

## ChMS Optimization & Training [.h2 .font-bold .font-secondary]

Fewer than 40% of church staff fully utilize the ChMS features available to them. This isn't a staff failure — it's a systems and training failure. Most ChMS implementations are rushed at go-live with minimal training and no workflow configuration, and over time staff revert to spreadsheets and workarounds because the system wasn't set up to support their actual ministry workflows. GSI's Optimization & Training engagement is for churches that launched a ChMS 1–3 years ago and aren't getting their money's worth, where staff still use spreadsheets alongside the ChMS, where reporting is unreliable because data entry is inconsistent, or where a new executive pastor inherited a setup they don't fully understand. We deliver a feature-by-feature utilization audit, workflow reconfiguration to match how the church actually operates, report and dashboard setup for the metrics leadership actually needs, comprehensive data cleanup, role-based staff training using the church's real data and real workflows, and a phased feature adoption roadmap for the next 6–12 months. The outcome: measurable increase in ChMS feature utilization within 90 days, reliable reporting, staff confidence, and clean accurate data.

## ChMS Hosting & Infrastructure [.h2 .font-bold .font-secondary]

A significant number of churches — particularly larger congregations running Rock RMS — still manage their own hosting infrastructure, creating security exposure, operational risk, and maintenance burden most churches aren't equipped to handle without dedicated IT leadership. GSI's Hosting & Infrastructure service covers hosting environment assessment, cloud migration feasibility and cost modeling, independent hosting vendor selection, backup and disaster recovery planning with documented and tested procedures, security hardening including patch management and access control review, and ongoing uptime monitoring and infrastructure health review. This is for churches running Rock RMS on local servers, churches on legacy ACS or PowerChurch installations, multi-site churches whose infrastructure hasn't scaled with campus growth, and any church experiencing performance issues or downtime related to their hosting environment. The outcome: a secure, reliable hosting environment that doesn't depend on a volunteer IT person to keep running.
